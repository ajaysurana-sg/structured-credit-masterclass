SLIDE 14 · Risk Transfer Recognition (Accounting)               ★ CFO MAGNET
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

HOOK
  CFO question: did the assets actually leave my balance sheet?

TALKING POINTS
  • Legal True Sale ≠ Accounting Derecognition (two separate tests)
  • IFRS 9 / Ind AS 109 = substantial-risks-and-rewards test + control test +
    continuing-involvement test
  • Most SRT and partial-transfer structures FAIL full derecognition
    because originator retains the equity tranche
  • Three outcomes:
      → Full Derecognition (rare)
      → Partial (common in SRT — continuing involvement at fair value)
      → No Derecognition (secured-borrowing accounting)
  • Implication: capital relief may be partial, not total

QUOTE
  "Legal isolation is necessary; accounting derecognition is the prize."

SLIDE 16 · Eligibility Criteria (Mechanism 2)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

HOOK
  First line of defence. Applied before any loan sees a tranche.

TALKING POINTS — THREE LAYERS
  Borrower-level:
    • FICO/CIBIL 700+
    • DTI cap 40-50%
    • Employment seasoning 12+ months
    • Bureau-clean, KYC + AML

  Loan-level:
    • LTV cap 75-80%
    • Current status (no delinquency)
    • Seasoning (RBI MHP 3-12 months)
    • Max loan size, maturity within stated tenor

  Pool-level:
    • Geographic concentration <25% any state
    • Sectoral cap
    • Single-borrower ceiling
    • Weighted-average targets
    • Replenishment criteria

  • Post-IL&FS RBI's 2021 Master Direction codified all of this
  • Closed the originate-to-distribute loophole

QUOTE
  "Only good loans get in. The first defence is at the gate."

SLIDE 17 · Static vs Replenishing Pools
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

HOOK
  Pools either run off or recycle — different SPV engineering.

TALKING POINTS
  • Amortizing (auto loans, mortgages) — balance steadily declines
  • Principal collections pass through to investors, paying down bonds
  • Revolving (credit cards, LOC) — short-term assets
  • Revolving Period: principal recycled to buy NEW loans, pool size constant
  • End of revolving period → controlled amortisation phase
  • Cannot structure a credit card portfolio like a mortgage pool

QUOTE
  "Behavior dictates engineering — not the reverse."

SLIDE 19 · Servicers — Master/Primary vs Special/Workout
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

HOOK
  SPV is a legal ghost. Servicers do all the actual work.

TALKING POINTS
  • Master/Primary Servicer = routine administration
  • Collects monthly payments, manages escrows, passes cash to SPV
  • The "good weather" operator
  • Special/Workout Servicer = covenants breached or defaulted
  • Authority to restructure debt, extend maturity, force liquidation
  • In India: ARCs operate under SARFAESI for secured + IBC for unsecured
  • US CMBS uses "Master/Special"; broader market often "Primary/Special"

SLIDE 25 · Worked Example — Subordination Math
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

HOOK

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

HOOK

  • Workhorse for European banks pre-2008
